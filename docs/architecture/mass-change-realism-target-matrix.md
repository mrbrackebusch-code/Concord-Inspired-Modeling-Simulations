# Mass & Change Realism Target Matrix

## Purpose

This note defines the required realism level for each of the six `Mass & Change` simulations.

It exists to prevent quality drift.

The project now needs an explicit answer to this question:

- what level of visual/process realism is required before any given simulation can be called done?

Without that answer, the suite can slowly collapse into lower-effort motion, weaker process detail, and "good enough for now" shortcuts that are incompatible with the larger project vision.

This file is the acceptance matrix that protects against that.

## Non-Negotiable Framing

These simulations are no longer being built as isolated lesson widgets with a future polish pass deferred indefinitely.

They are being built to survive inside a larger game/world shell.

That means:

- the quality bar rises now
- placeholder-level process is not an acceptable resting state
- each simulation must hit its pre-declared realism target before it is considered done

## Reality Tiers

### `R5` Hero Reality

Use this when the process is one of the visual centerpieces of the suite.

Required traits:

- highly readable real-world staging
- strong material identity throughout the process
- rich secondary motion and secondary detail
- measurement/readout behavior tied into the event
- no major frame that reads as "cheap effect"
- strong enough to feel satisfying even inside a broader game/world shell

### `R4` High Reality

Use this when the process is not the single most spectacular one, but still must feel premium and fully intentional.

Required traits:

- clear reality-first staging
- strong object/material read
- meaningful secondary motion
- convincing process continuity
- no shorthand that breaks trust
- strong enough to stand next to `R5` work without collapsing into obvious placeholder quality

### `R3` Baseline Reality

This is included for taxonomy only.

It means:

- clear process
- believable enough for a simpler educational simulation
- less secondary detail

For the current `Mass & Change` suite:

- `R3` is **not sufficient for shipping**

Now that the broader project shell exists, no `Mass & Change` simulation should be considered done below `R4`.

## Global Shipping Floor For This Suite

Every one of the six simulations must meet all of the following:

1. The process is based on a real-world reference target.
2. The process is readable in early, middle, and late stages.
3. Matter has continuity and does not teleport or magically reclassify.
4. Orientation, contact, and gravity cues are shown when relevant.
5. The scale/readout behaves as part of the event, not as a static label.
6. The single fakest frame has been identified and improved.
7. The process survives direct motion review, not just stills.
8. The object art is strong enough to stand inside the larger game/world shell.

If any of those fail, the simulation is not done regardless of the nominal tier.

## Per-Simulation Targets

### 1. Steel Wool Burns

Required tier: `R5`

Why:

- this is one of the visual centerpieces of the suite
- it is the hardest material transformation in the set
- it sets the ceiling for how "alive" the project can feel

Reality beats that must exist:

1. intact metallic tangled wool before ignition
2. localized ignition point
3. first glow in one region, not the whole sample
4. irregular sparks from active burn regions
5. attached flame behavior
6. glow spreading through the wool
7. progressive darkening / oxidized residue
8. final object still reading as the same sample, transformed
9. measured mass rising during the burn

Secondary detail required:

- slight timing irregularity in sparks/glow
- non-uniform burn across the sample
- residue texture changing during the event
- optional faint smoke / heat shimmer if it improves realism

Unacceptable shortcuts:

- generic flame icon pasted on top
- full-sample instant glow
- perfectly even burn
- all sparks identical
- mass changing only at the end

Not done until:

- the burn reads as oxidation moving through tangled metal
- the scale feels involved in the event
- the scene could reasonably serve as a visual benchmark for the suite

### 2. Precipitate

Required tier: `R5`

Why:

- it is one of the most process-rich experiences in the suite
- transfer, mixing, clouding, and settling all need to be convincing
- this is the clearest test of whether the repo can do "observed reality" instead of storyboard tricks

Reality target:

- actual chemistry target: `Ca(NO3)2 + Na2CO3 -> CaCO3`
- visually, this means clear solutions giving way to a white/cloudy precipitate process

Reality beats that must exist:

1. believable source and receiver apparatus
2. receiver positioned in a believable way for transfer
3. source tube lift
4. source tube tilt
5. liquid slosh toward the lip before main transfer
6. readable main stream
7. receiving liquid visibly disturbed by the incoming stream
8. cloud begins where mixing occurs
9. cloud swirls and densifies
10. settling / bottom accumulation late in the process
11. final drips that complete the transfer
12. before/after weighing as separate measurement moments

Secondary detail required:

- slight stream variation
- timing difference between first contact and full clouding
- non-uniform cloud distribution
- lingering fine suspended matter before full settling

Unacceptable shortcuts:

- tube tilts and the result is already mixed
- straight-line pour with no slosh
- cloud appearing all at once
- static fog overlay with no internal behavior

Not done until:

- the learner can tell exactly where the new solid/cloud came from by watching
- the transfer feels complete
- the mixing zone is the visual cause of the precipitate

### 3. Alka-Seltzer

Required tier: `R5`

Why:

- it is the suite's strongest bubbling/gas-process opportunity
- it has the most obvious teenager-appeal potential after burning
- it benefits heavily from rich secondary detail

Reality beats that must exist:

1. tablet visibly present before reaction
2. readable onset delay
3. first small bubble release near the tablet
4. bubble count and size increasing over time
5. visible liquid disturbance around the tablet
6. continued bubble escape or accumulation behavior depending on setup
7. taper as the reaction winds down
8. measurement tied to the event

Secondary detail required:

- varied bubble sizes
- different bubble paths
- local fizz density near the tablet
- possible headspace gas behavior in captured mode

Unacceptable shortcuts:

- instant full fizz scene
- bubbles appearing everywhere at once
- no visible source region
- label-based differentiation instead of visible gas behavior

Not done until:

- the reaction feels lively and materially satisfying
- a student would want to watch the process run again
- vented/captured differences are visible rather than merely stated

### 4. Sugar Dissolves

Required tier: `R4`

Why:

- less spectacular than burn or precipitate
- but still needs to feel intentional, rich, and reality-based

Reality beats that must exist:

1. sugar mass/granules visible before dissolving
2. uneven early dissolution
3. visible concentration/plume behavior
4. slower unstirred behavior
5. faster stirred behavior
6. final disappearance from sight without implying disappearance of matter
7. stable measurement logic

Secondary detail required:

- grain-level soft loss
- cloudy or translucent concentration zones
- different fluid behavior between stirred and unstirred

Unacceptable shortcuts:

- sugar simply fading away
- stirred and unstirred differing only in duration label
- no intermediate cloudiness or fluid effect

Not done until:

- the student can feel the difference between slow passive dissolving and more active mixing
- the loss of visibility feels earned, not toggled

### 5. Ice To Water

Required tier: `R4`

Why:

- simpler than burn or precipitate
- but still a crucial test of whether the repo can do restrained realism rather than icon shrink

Reality beats that must exist:

1. multiple distinct ice blocks at the start
2. blocks visibly reading as separate blocks
3. softening / wetting / gloss change
4. runoff contributing to water level
5. rounding / sloughing of the blocks
6. late oblong or rounded remnants before disappearance
7. clock and readout supporting the process

Secondary detail required:

- subtle surface glint changes
- non-identical melting of the blocks
- water surface behavior responding over time

Unacceptable shortcuts:

- three cubes scaling down in place
- instantaneous puddle reveal
- final stage still reading mainly as transparent ghost cubes

Not done until:

- the ice seems to lose sharpness and solidity in a way that feels observed
- the late-stage remnant reads as melting matter, not faded icon art

### 6. Steel Wool Pulled Apart

Required tier: `R4`

Why:

- this one is more tactile than flashy
- it still needs to feel physically convincing, not diagrammatic

Reality beats that must exist:

1. believable tangled wool before handling
2. one clear pull affordance
3. visible deformation under tension
4. visible separation of strands/material
5. slight settling or follow-through after release
6. scale involvement as part of the evidence path

Secondary detail required:

- subtle loose-fiber behavior
- slight misalignment / irregularity after pulling
- optional tiny flecks if they materially help

Unacceptable shortcuts:

- handle moves but wool does not materially respond
- the wool separates like a clean rubber shape
- return-to-scale feels like a state jump instead of continued evidence flow

Not done until:

- the learner feels like they actually handled tangled material
- the motion reads as manipulation of matter, not manipulation of an icon

## Rule For Future Work

A simulation is not done when:

- the JSON loads
- the buttons work
- there is some animation
- the result communicates the general idea

A simulation is done only when it satisfies its declared realism tier and clears the global shipping floor.
